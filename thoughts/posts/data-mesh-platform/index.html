What I didn’t share is that, I was working on an implementation of a Kubernetes Operator, to support this architecture in a self-serve manner. With the objective to define some organization concept such as Data Domain or Data Platform in Kubernetes (via a YAML manifest), and translate them in a concrete provisioning in Kubernetes and on Cloud provider (AWS, GCP).

Here is the simplified architecture I was working on. The architecture was mainly composed of a set of operator:

  • Dagster operator: responsible to interact with Kubernetes API and Dagster instances, to deploy and manage the lifecycle of Dagster User Deployment instance and Dagster Instance
  • Airbyte operator: same but for Airbyte
  • Datahub operator: same but for Datahubproject
  • Konpyutaikia operator: responsible to convert:
    • DataPlatform custom resource: in Dagster, Airbyte and Datahub custom resources, and Kubernetes resources, to setup the Data Platform
    • Domain custom resource: to translate a Domain definition in actions on the different platform components (e.g create a domain on Datahub, Deploy a Dagster user deployment or create an Airbyte namespace).

# In nutshell

The assumption in Data mesh is that data collection, experimentation, and intelligence are outsourced to a separate data team. The data team is under sheer pressure. Domains did not have trust in the data, or often couldn‚Äôt find the data they needed.

Data mesh is a decentralized sociotechnical approach to share, access, and manage analytical data in complex and large-scale environments - within or across organizations. Moving from:

  • Centralized ownership -> decentralized domain ownership
  • Monolithic -> distributed architecture
  • Data by product of -> Data and code as one unit
  • Top-down governance -> federated computational governance
  • Data as an asset to collect -> Data as a product to share and connect
  • Fragmented platform (analytics & operational) -> Integrated platform

# The principles

There is 4 principles that drives the data mesh:

  • Domain-oriented ownership: Decentralize the ownership of analytical data to business domains closest to the data - either the source of the data or its main consumers.
  • Data as a Product: The data is shared as a product directly with data users across domains
  • Federated Computation Governance: The approach is to delegates the responsibility of modeling data and quality to individual domains, and heavily automates the computational instructions. Its a decision making model led by Data Product owners, platform experts and legal & security representatives.
  • Self-serve data platform: Services that empower domain’s cross-functional teams to share data. The platform services are centered around removing friction from then end-to-end journey of data sharing, from source to consumption. The platform services manage the full lifecycle of individual data products.

With this in mind, I’m shifting my approach, from building a platform that will support the data management, to a platform that will support an organization to share data through Data Product at scale.
